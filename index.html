Morning Mobility

Why this routine?

Hockey + sitting = tight hip flexors and a shortened psoas. This causes knee pain, lower back issues, and reduced stride power over time. This routine targets the root cause, not just the symptoms.

The psoas muscle

The psoas connects your spine to your legs and is the most important muscle you've probably never trained. When it shortens from sitting or skating in a crouched position, everything else compensates. The daily routine here addresses this directly.

How to use this

Open each exercise, watch the video, then use the timer. Tap "Done" when finished. Dynamic exercises (before skating/running) warm the tissue. Static stretches (after) lengthen it. Both matter. Consistency over weeks beats intensity in one session.

Sequence matters

The exercises are ordered intentionally — dynamic movements before static holds. Don't skip ahead. The muscle needs to be warmed before it can be lengthened effectively.
